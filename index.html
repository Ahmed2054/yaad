    Our Constitution


    Name of Association
    YAAD Welfare

    Founders
    YAAD Welfare was founded by the following members:
    Yeboah Kobena Eric Arhin Joseph Ahmed Ofosu Dugan Christopher
    The organization was established on 30th September, 2023 with the primary goal of promoting savings among its members and providing support through loans at affordable rates to its members.
    About
    The association's name, YAAD Welfare, was derived from the initials of the four founders' names.YAAD Welfare is a welfare association with the primary objective of encouraging its members to be disciplined in saving a portion of their monthly income for 11 months each year. The saved amount is repaid to the members at the end of the year. The associationi also provides loans at reduced rates for its members, who additionally benefit from the interest earned on loans over the year. Non-members can also access loans but at a higher rate.

    1. Membership Categories
    a. Members
    i. Paid registration fee.
    ii. Make regular monthly contributions.

    b. Non-Members
    i. Can only take loans from the association.
    2. Registration Fee
    a. Annual Fee: GHS 50.00
    3. Contributions
    a. Monthly Contributions:
    i. The contribution amount is equal for all months.
    ii. Members determine their own monthly contribution amount.
    iii. Contributions are made from January to November each year.

    4. Services
    a. Loans
    5. Interest Rate
    a. Members: 5% per month
    b. Members have maximum of two (2) months to repay the loan contracted.
    c. Non-Members: 8% per month
    d. Non-members have maximum of one (1) month to repay the loan contracted.

    6. Penalty
    a. Failure to repay the loan in full by the due date will attract a penalty.
    b. The penalty amount is equal to the interest amount on the loan.

    7. Financial Reporting Year
    a. A financial report is issued to members every December.

    8. End-of-Year Benefit
    a. Members are entitled to a share of the interest earned over the period.
    b. The interest is distributed based on the ratio of each memberâ€™s registration fee and accumulated contributions for the period.

    9. Payment of Entitlement
    a. Members receive their entitlements at the end of the financial reporting year.
    b. Entitlements include:
    i. Registration fee
    ii. Accumulated contributions
    iii. End-of-year benefit
